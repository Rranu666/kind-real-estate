The 2024 NAR Settlement Explained: What California Buyers & Sellers Must Know Now

On August 17, 2024, the most significant change to US real estate in decades quietly took effect. The National Association of Realtors (NAR) $418 million settlement fundamentally rewrote the rules of how buyer's agents are compensated — and if you're buying or selling in California, this directly affects how much you pay and what rights you now have.

What actually changed: Before the settlement, seller's agents typically offered buyer's agent commissions (usually 2.5–3%) through the MLS, effectively baked into the listing price. Sellers rarely knew exactly what they were paying buyer's agents. That system is now prohibited. Buyer rep agreements are now mandatory before any showing — buyers must sign a written agreement specifying exactly how their agent will be compensated, and for how much.

What this means for California buyers: You now have the right to negotiate your buyer's agent fee directly. You can ask for a flat fee, an hourly rate, or a percentage — and you can compare agents on price just like any other service. The old "the seller pays so it's free for me" myth is dead. Commissions were always baked into the home price. Now they're transparent.

What this means for California sellers: You're no longer obligated to offer buyer's agent compensation through the MLS. You can still choose to offer it as an incentive — and many sellers will — but it's now a negotiation, not a default. In competitive markets like Beverly Hills or San Francisco, offering buyer's agent compensation may help attract more buyers. In slower markets like parts of the Central Valley, you have leverage to reduce it or eliminate it entirely.

How Kind Real Estate responds: We built our platform specifically for this new world. Our 2.5% flat commission covers the full transaction. Every fee is disclosed upfront. No hidden buyer's agent splits. No inflated "traditional" costs. Our Buyer Education Centre (free, takes 20 minutes) will get you fully versed in your new rights — and earn you a Buyer Certified™ badge that signals to sellers you're a serious, prepared buyer.
